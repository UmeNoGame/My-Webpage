From February to July 2024, I had the incredible opportunity to spend five months in Cambodia as part of a civic service program with the association Les Enfants du Sourire Khmer. It was my first time traveling so far, for so long, and on my own. This experience pushed me out of my comfort zone, allowing me to discover a new country, a new culture, and most importantly, a new side of myself.

At the educational center for children, my tasks were varied and rewarding. I taught English, led sports and creative activities, helped manage the center, and organized outings for the children. Each day brought new challenges and special moments, and working closely with the children taught me patience, creativity, and the importance of providing a safe and inspiring environment for learning.

Living in Cambodia also gave me the chance to improve my Khmer to an intermediate level, which made daily interactions much more meaningful. Beyond the language, I was fully immersed in Cambodian culture ‚Äì from local traditions and food to community values. Experiencing this way of life firsthand opened my eyes to new perspectives and left a lasting impression on me.

This experience was truly transformative. Traveling alone, exploring a new country, and contributing to the children's growth gave me skills, memories, and a broader outlook that I couldn't have gained otherwise. I return home inspired, motivated, and eager to continue learning, traveling, and engaging in meaningful projects that make a difference.
